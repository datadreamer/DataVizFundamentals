Evaluating Data.


Dimensionality.


A measure of the number of variable properties of a given object or data sample.

Range.


The measure between the minimum and maximum values for a given variable property.

Granularity.


The resolution of a set of data samples or the possible values within a variable property.

Transposing Data.


The process of translating data into a form that can be experienced through senses (visible, audible, tangible).

Data Variables &
Visual Variables.


Number of Variables.


Match properties in your data to visual properties in your visualization (ie: position, size, color, value, etc).

Range of Variables.


Choose an appropriate visual variable that scales properly with the range of your data field (ie: black to white, color over spectrum, width/height, etc).

Comparison Between Variables.


Consider accommodating for unexpected patterns to appear between different variables, and choosing the right visual representation to present comparisons.

Design System.


Design as Template.


Create a visualization to accommodate changing content over time.

Preparing for the Unexpected.


Prepare your visualization to accept results that may be out of the range of your current ones.

Comparisons Over Time.


Ensure consistency in your visualization in order to make comparisons between today's data and yesterday's data.
